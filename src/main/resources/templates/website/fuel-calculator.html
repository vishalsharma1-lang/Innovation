Fuel Cost Guide

Petrol vs Diesel Running Cost

Diesel vehicles are typically more fuel-efficient (18–22 kmpl vs 13–17 kmpl for petrol) and diesel is cheaper per litre. However, diesel engines cost ₹1–2L more upfront. Break-even usually happens at 60,000–80,000 km.

  • Petrol: Best for city drives under 10,000 km/year
  • Diesel: Better for highway/high-mileage users (15,000+ km/year)

CNG Running Cost Guide

CNG (Compressed Natural Gas) is 50–60% cheaper than petrol per km. A CNG car running 1,500 km/month saves ₹60,000–80,000 per year vs petrol.

  • CNG mileage: 20–28 km/kg (depends on engine)
  • Delhi CNG: ~₹75/kg · Mumbai: ~₹74/kg
  • Ideal for taxis, high-mileage city commuters

EV Running Cost Guide

Electric vehicles cost just ₹1–2 per km at home charging rates, making them 70–80% cheaper than petrol. The savings on 1,500 km/month can be ₹80,000–1,00,000/year.

  • Home charging: ₹6–10/kWh (cheapest)
  • Public fast chargers: ₹15–25/kWh
  • Efficiency: 5–8 km/kWh (Nexon EV: ~6.5 km/kWh)

How Fuel Cost is Calculated

Formula: Cost per km = Fuel Price ÷ Mileage

  • Petrol: ₹94.72 ÷ 15 kmpl = ₹6.31/km
  • Diesel: ₹87.62 ÷ 18 kmpl = ₹4.87/km
  • CNG: ₹75.09 ÷ 22 km/kg = ₹3.41/km
  • EV: ₹8.00 ÷ 6.5 km/kWh = ₹1.23/km
Frequently Asked Questions
Which fuel is cheapest to run in India?
EV (electric) is cheapest at ₹1–2/km. CNG is second at ₹3–4/km. Diesel is around ₹4–6/km. Petrol is the most expensive at ₹6–8/km. However, higher upfront cost and infrastructure availability must be considered before switching.
How do I find my car's actual mileage?
Real-world mileage is 15–20% lower than ARAI-certified figures due to traffic, AC usage, load, and driving style. Fill a full tank, drive 200–300 km, refill and calculate: KM driven ÷ Litres consumed = your real mileage.
Why do fuel prices differ across Indian cities?
Petrol and diesel prices include state VAT which varies from 20–35%. States like Rajasthan, Telangana and Maharashtra charge higher VAT, making fuel more expensive there. CNG prices are set by city gas distribution companies and electricity tariffs are regulated by state electricity boards.
Is it worth switching from Petrol to CNG?
If you drive 1,200+ km/month, a CNG retrofit (₹60,000–80,000) typically pays back within 12–18 months. You'll save ₹4,000–6,000/month vs petrol. Trade-offs: reduced boot space, slightly less power, and CNG stations outside major cities are limited.
How often are fuel prices updated?
Petrol and diesel prices are revised daily by oil marketing companies (IOC, HPCL, BPCL) though actual changes are infrequent. CNG prices are revised every 2–4 months. This calculator uses approximate current retail prices — verify on your fuel company's website for exact today's price.
How is EV charging cost calculated?
EV cost per km = Electricity tariff (₹/kWh) ÷ Efficiency (km/kWh). At Delhi's domestic rate of ₹8/kWh and Nexon EV's 6.5 km/kWh efficiency: ₹8 ÷ 6.5 = ₹1.23/km. Always use your home slab rate for accurate calculation — public fast chargers can cost 2–3× more.
